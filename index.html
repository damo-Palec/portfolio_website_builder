How to use DAMSAN Portfolio Studio

Fill the fields on the left — the preview updates live. When it looks right, click Download your website and publish it (steps at the bottom).

The fields

  • Your name / Role & location — shown in the header and the hero line.
  • Hero headline / subtext — the big intro on the Home page.
  • Story video URL — paste a YouTube, Vimeo, Google Drive, Loom or MP4 link. It adds a Watch My Story button under your photo. Leave blank to hide it.
  • Photo URL — an image link, a Google Drive share link, or a local filename like photo.jpg uploaded next to your site. If it's blank or the image can't load, your initials are shown instead (e.g. DP).
  • Home stats strip — one per line as Value :: Label (e.g. 15+ :: Years of experience).
  • Email / Phone / LinkedIn — shown on the Contact page as tappable links.
  • Resume / CV link — a local file like resume.pdf, a Drive link, or any URL. Adds a Download Resume button.
  • Accent color — the highlight colour for buttons, links and headings.
  • About — one paragraph per blank line.
  • What I do (services) — one per line as Title :: description.
  • Expertise — one group per line as Category :: item, item, item.

Projects (the detailed part)

Each project is a block, and blocks are separated by a line with ===. Inside a block:

  • First line: TAG :: Title :: link (the link is optional).
  • Challenge: one or two sentences.
  • Solution: one or two sentences.
  • Impact: items separated by a pipe — a | b | c.
  • Tech: items separated by a pipe — a | b | c.
  • Video: a YouTube, Vimeo, Drive, Loom or MP4 link (optional). Adds a video button on the project card that opens a player.

Each project becomes a clickable card; clicking it opens a popup with Challenge → Solution → Impact → Technologies.

Videos

There are two ways to add video, and both open a built-in player overlay:

  • Watch My Story — fill the Story video URL field to add a button under your hero photo.
  • Project videos — add a Video: line inside any project block. That project's card then shows a small video icon next to “View details”.

To watch: click the video icon (or the Watch My Story button) — a player opens; close it with × or the Esc key. To add a video: paste any YouTube, Vimeo, Google Drive, Loom, or MP4 link. Leave it blank to hide the button.

Toolbar & view controls

The icons at the top of the builder are labelled beneath each one:

  • Help (?) — opens this guide.
  • Video (▶) — plays a short tutorial on how to use the builder.
  • Default (home) — restores the original default content.
  • Undo — brings back everything after a Clear (it lights up right after you clear).
  • Clear (reset) — empties every field in one click; use Undo beside it to bring it all back.
  • Expand (⤢) — makes the builder fill the screen, hiding the preview, for easier typing.
  • Hide (☰, in the preview bar) — hides the builder for a full-width preview of your site.

Sample profiles

Click Sample to load a ready-made profile for a learning role — Instructional Designer, eLearning Developer, Motion Graphics Designer, LMS Administrator, Learning Experience Designer, Learning Experience Producer, AI Learning Technology Specialist, or Head of Learning Experience Centre. Each fills every field with realistic content you can customise. It replaces your current fields, so use Export first if you want to keep them.

Save & reuse your work

  • Export — saves your filled fields to a file, for review, sharing, or backup.
  • Import — loads a saved file and refills every field instantly, so you never retype.
  • Template — a blank file to hand to other people to fill in.
  • Copy / Code — copy the website's HTML, or view it.

Publish on GitHub (free hosting)

1Click Download your website to save your index.html. Also keep any local files you referenced (like photo.jpg or resume.pdf) in the same folder.
2Create a free account at github.com.
3Click the + at the top right → New repository. Give it a name (e.g. my-portfolio), set it to Public, and click Create repository.
4On the new repo page, click uploading an existing file. Drag in your index.html and any photo/resume files together, then click Commit changes.
5Open Settings → Pages. Under Branch, choose main and / (root), then Save.
6Wait 1–2 minutes. Your live site appears at https://your-username.github.io/my-portfolio/.
7To update later: change your fields here, download again, and re-upload index.html to the repo (replace the old one). The live site refreshes automatically.

Tip: before downloading, use Export to save your data — next time, Import it to pick up exactly where you left off.
